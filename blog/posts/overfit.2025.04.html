Overfitting

By Em • June 2025

Intro

During the development of a machine learning model, data is given to it for it to learn from, this is similar to how a person may study, one might study by revising a sample test then cross checking the answers. Over time the model may gradually become very good at doing that particular set of tests, it may even score 100% accuracy when validated, however, the caveat is when presented with a new test and no answers, it might fail to reproduce any usable answers. This is referred to as a generalization problem, or more correctly, the model has overfit the data. Using the test-answer analogy, it would be similar to a student studying a particular paper, but not learning how to reason about the problem, so when faced with a new set of problems, they begin to struggle.

“Adapt, improvise, overcome.” — Bear Grylls
